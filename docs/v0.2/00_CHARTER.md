# Kovacs V0.2.0 Charter

Status: normative

## Objective

Prove that Kovacs can remain visibly present during a user-started Windows work session, observe authorized screen activity, maintain awareness of the daily objective, and deliver timely coding and focus guidance through an always-on-top pet without retaining raw captures or taking external actions.

## Product contract

- The pet may launch idle with Windows, but capture begins only after `Start Day`.
- Start Day asks for today's objective and relates it to the durable main goal: Elite AI Systems Staff Engineer, with OpenAI as the efficiency benchmark.
- Observation is local, bounded, visibly indicated, allowlisted, and pausable.
- Raw frames exist only in memory or invocation-scoped temporary storage and are deleted after use.
- Kovacs advises and drafts. It does not click, type, send, submit, publish, commit, or modify the target project.
- V0.1 remains the reasoning, privacy, assistance, schema-validation, and audit kernel.

## Included

- Windows 10/11 desktop pet;
- explicit Start Day, Pause, Resume, Private, Observe Now, and End Day controls;
- daily objective and main-goal context;
- active-window authorization;
- screen-change sampling and in-memory comparison;
- ephemeral screenshot attachment to `codex exec`;
- coding and focus events;
- graduated normal, important, and critical interventions;
- minimum call interval and single-flight reasoning;
- structured event audit with no raw image data;
- end-of-day V0.1 debrief;
- secure Electron renderer and narrow IPC bridge.

## Excluded

- microphone or system-audio capture;
- meeting intelligence beyond future caption adapters;
- career-site adapters, CV submission, and social publishing;
- autonomous computer control;
- hidden or undetectable operation;
- raw screenshot retention by default;
- cloud synchronization;
- continuous video streaming;
- automatic permanent promotion of consequential conclusions.

## Release rule

V0.2.0 passes only when all automated metrics pass and a Windows pet smoke session proves visible capture state, authorized observation, intervention display, pause/private enforcement, raw-frame deletion, and V0.1 regression compatibility.
